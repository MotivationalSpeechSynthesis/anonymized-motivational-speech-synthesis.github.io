Motivational Speech Synthesis

Motivational speech has emerged as a prominent cultural phenomenon, characterized by persuasive rhetoric, emotional intensity, and a focus on success-driven narratives. This paper presents a computational approach to replicating the distinctive prosodic patterns of motivational speech while critically examining its sociocultural underpinnings. Our research builds on recent advancements in speech emotion recognition and emotion control in text-to-speech (TTS). Leveraging open sources deep learning models techniques, we analyze and enhance web-scraped motivational speech data. The generated dataset helps to construct a TTS architecture capable of synthesizing motivational speech with adjustable intensity. Our proposed model, adapted from EmoKnob, implements a motivational factor, a one-dimensional scale derived from high-dimensional emotional speech representations. By situating motivational speech within broader discourses of self-optimization and meritocracy, this project advances emotional speech synthesis while inviting reflection on the societal values embedded in such mediated narratives.
