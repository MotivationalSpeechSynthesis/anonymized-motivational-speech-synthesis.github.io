Motivational speech has emerged as a prominent cultural phenomenon, characterized by persuasive rhetoric, emotional intensity, and a focus on success-driven narratives. This paper presents a computational approach to replicating the distinctive prosodic patterns of motivational speech while critically examining its sociocultural underpinnings. Our research builds on recent advancements in speech emotion recognition and emotion control in text-to-speech (TTS). Leveraging open sources deep learning models techniques, we analyze and enhance web-scraped motivational speech data. The generated dataset helps to construct a TTS architecture capable of synthesizing motivational speech with adjustable intensity. Our proposed model, adapted from EmoKnob, implements a motivational factor, a one-dimensional scale derived from high-dimensional emotional speech representations. By situating motivational speech within broader discourses of self-optimization and meritocracy, this project advances emotional speech synthesis while inviting reflection on the societal values embedded in such mediated narratives.

Proposed architecture

The following diagram illustrates the architecture developed for synthesizing motivational speech building ontop the EmoKnob model. Motivational intensity is controlled via averaged speaker embeddings, derived by selecting and averaging speech samples corresponding to different levels of motivational intensity from our dataset. These embeddings are generated in discrete increments along our one-dimensional motivational factor, ranging from 0 (low intensity) to 1 (high intensity). During inference, the closest embedding is selected according to the desired motivational factor, enabling fine-grained emotional control of synthesized speech.

Motivational Factor

The visualization below shows a 3-dimensional representation of emotional speech samples extracted from motivational speeches, projected into a Valence-Arousal-Dominance (VAD) space. Each point represents a motivational speech segment collected from YouTube, embedded using a deep-learning-based emotion recognition model (Wav2Vec). Using the UMAP dimensionality reduction algorithm, these complex emotional patterns are compressed into a single value, called the motivational factor, which ranges from 0 (low motivational intensity) to 1 (high motivational intensity). Explore the interactive plot to see how emotional dimensions correspond to the motivational intensity scale.
